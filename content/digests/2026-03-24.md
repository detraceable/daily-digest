### Air Street Capital Becomes Europe's Largest Solo VC with $232M Fund
London-based [Air Street Capital has raised a $232 million Fund III](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/air-street-becomes-one-of-the-largest-solo-vcs-in-europe-with-232m-fund/), establishing itself as one of Europe's largest solo venture capital firms. Led by Nathan Benaich, the fund will focus on early-stage AI companies across Europe and North America with check sizes ranging from $500,000 to $15 million, and select growth investments up to $25 million. Air Street has already backed notable AI unicorns like Black Forest Labs and ElevenLabs, while seeing successful exits from companies like Adept (acquired by Amazon) and Graphcore (sold to SoftBank). With this latest raise, Air Street now manages $400 million in assets, having significantly scaled from its $17 million Fund I in 2020 to $121 million for Fund II, demonstrating the accelerating investor interest in AI infrastructure and applications.

### Critical iPhone Exploit Kit Leaked, Endangering Millions of Users
A potentially devastating security situation has emerged as [someone has publicly leaked an advanced exploit kit called DarkSword](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/23/someone-has-publicly-leaked-an-exploit-kit-that-can-hack-millions-of-iphones/) that can hack millions of iPhones. Security researchers warn that the leaked code, published on GitHub, allows anyone to easily target iPhone users running older iOS versions. The exploit, previously used by Russian government hackers against Ukrainian targets, works by creating a webpage that can read and exfiltrate sensitive data including contacts, messages, call history, and stored passwords from affected devices. According to security expert Matthias Frielingsdorf, "The exploits will work out of the box... There is no iOS expertise required," making this an unusually accessible attack vector. Apple has issued emergency updates for affected devices and recommends all users update their software immediately, as updated iOS 26 devices and those with Lockdown Mode enabled remain protected.

Anthropic has taken a significant step forward in AI capability with a [new update to Claude that allows it to autonomously control users' computers](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/899430/anthropic-claude-code-cowork-ai-control-computer). ### FCC Bans Import of Foreign-Made Consumer Routers Citing Security Risks
In a major regulatory move that will reshape the networking equipment market, the [FCC has banned the import of new consumer routers manufactured overseas](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/fcc-bans-import-of-new-consumer-routers-made-overseas-citing-security-risks/), citing significant national security concerns. FCC Commissioner Brendan Carr announced that all future routers made in foreign countries will be prohibited from entering the United States unless manufacturers receive specific exemptions. This follows a similar December ban on foreign-made drones. While existing routers can continue to be used and companies with FCC approval can still sell their inventory, this decision will dramatically impact supply chains and potentially increase costs for home and small business networking equipment. The move reflects growing concerns about backdoors and vulnerabilities in networking equipment that could be exploited by foreign actors.

### $23M Resolv Hack Shows Dangers of Off-Chain Infrastructure Compromise
A sophisticated [attack on the Resolv DeFi protocol resulted in $23 million being stolen](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/lessons-from-the-resolv-hack/) in a matter of minutes through a critical security breach. Rather than exploiting smart contract vulnerabilities, attackers compromised Resolv's AWS Key Management Service environment to gain access to a privileged signing key used for minting the protocol's USR stablecoin. With this key, the attackers authorized the minting of 80 million unbacked tokens despite only depositing $100-200K in collateral. The incident highlights a growing security concern in DeFi: while significant attention is paid to on-chain security, off-chain components like cloud infrastructure and privileged keys represent an expanding attack surface that can lead to catastrophic failures even when smart contracts function as intended. Real-time on-chain monitoring tools might have detected and prevented the abnormal minting activity before the attacker could extract the full value.
