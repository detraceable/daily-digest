### CERN Pioneers Ultra-Compact AI Models for Real-Time Physics Discoveries
[CERN uses tiny AI models burned into silicon for real-time LHC data filtering](https://theopenreader.org/Journalism:CERN_Uses_Tiny_AI_Models_Burned_into_Silicon_for_Real-Time_LHC_Data_Filtering) details how the organization has developed specialized AI systems physically embedded in custom silicon to process the Large Hadron Collider's massive data output. Unlike conventional GPU or TPU-based AI systems, these ultra-optimized models work directly within field-programmable gate arrays (FPGAs) and application-specific integrated circuits (ASICs) to make split-second decisions at the detector level—determining which collision events have scientific value and which should be discarded, all within nanoseconds. This hardware-first approach, which includes extensive precomputed lookup tables to deliver near-instantaneous outputs for common detector signals, enables CERN to filter the LHC's overwhelming data stream of hundreds of terabytes per second down to approximately one petabyte of valuable scientific data daily.

### AMD Doubles Down on 3D V-Cache with Ryzen 9950X3D2 Dual Edition
[AMD's Ryzen 9 9950X3D2 Dual Edition crams 208MB of cache into a single chip](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2026/03/amds-ryzen-9-9950x3d2-dual-edition-crams-208mb-of-cache-into-a-single-chip/) introduces a groundbreaking processor that solves the hybrid arrangement limitations of previous X3D chips. Unlike earlier models that placed 3D V-Cache on only one of their two CPU chiplets, the 9950X3D2 features 64MB of stacked cache on both dies, delivering a staggering total of 208MB (including 16MB L2 and 64MB built-in L3 cache). This architecture eliminates the complex software scheduling required by previous hybrid designs, which occasionally suffered from core parking issues, while promising up to 10% performance gains in cache-dependent applications. Though the chip comes with slight tradeoffs in clock speed (5.6GHz vs 5.7GHz) and higher power consumption (200W vs 170W TDP), it represents the culmination of AMD's refinement of its 3D stacking technology, offering a fully overclockable solution when it launches on April 22.
