### Energy Innovation Scales Up
Arbor Energy has secured a [billion-dollar order to bring rocket turbine tech to the power grid](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/arbor-energy-just-landed-a-billion-dollar-order-to-bring-rocket-turbine-tech-to-the-power-grid/), demonstrating how specialized engineering from aerospace is revolutionizing terrestrial energy production. The company's Halcyon turbines, derived from rocket turbomachinery and manufactured through 3D printing, will provide GridMarket with up to 5 gigawatts of capacity. While originally designed to run carbon-negative by consuming biomass, the system has been modified to accept natural gas as well, highlighting the pragmatic balance between innovative clean technology and market demands. With data centers driving unprecedented power requirements and traditional manufacturers struggling to meet timelines, Arbor exemplifies how startups can leverage modern manufacturing techniques to bypass traditional supply chain constraints.

### Space-Grade Software Transforms Manufacturing
Former SpaceX engineers are bringing [sophisticated software solutions from the rocket world to factory floors](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/with-sift-stack-two-ex-spacex-engineers-are-bringing-the-software-that-helped-launch-rockets-to-the-factory-floor/) through their company Sift Stack. What started as a tool to manage telemetry data from spacecraft has rapidly evolved to become infrastructure for the AI revolution in manufacturing. The El Segundo-based startup, which raised a $42 million Series B at a $274 million valuation, helps companies like satellite-maker Astranis organize and analyze the massive data streams from today's software-intensive machines. Sift's pivot highlights the industry-wide shift toward data infrastructure that can support AI decision-making in manufacturing processes, aligning with the broader "atoms, not bits" movement championed by investors like Jeff Bezos, who's reportedly assembling a $100 billion fund focused on factory automation.

### Fintech Pivots to Success
Y Combinator graduate [Glimpse has secured $35 million in Series A funding](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/after-pivoting-y-combinator-grad-glimpse-raises-35m-led-by-a16z/) led by Andreessen Horowitz after completely reinventing its business. The company's journey from Airbnb product placements to dispute-tracking automation for retailers demonstrates the value of flexibility and market observation in the startup world. Glimpse's platform now helps over 200 brands, including major names like Suave and Chapstick, automate the tedious process of tracking and disputing financial deductions that retailers take from invoices. By combining AI agents with human oversight, the system identifies invalid deductions that might otherwise result in significant revenue leakage for consumer packaged goods companies. This successful pivot underscores how founders Akash Raju, Anuj Mehta, and Kushal Negi transformed market insights into a venture-backed solution addressing a specific but widespread pain point.

### Wine 11 Supercharges Windows Gaming on Linux
The latest iteration of [Wine 11 represents a fundamental reimagining of how Linux runs Windows games](https://www.xda-developers.com/wine-11-rewrites-linux-runs-windows-games-speed-gains/), promising dramatic performance improvements through a comprehensive rewrite of its kernel implementation. This transformative update could significantly reshape the gaming landscape by reducing the performance gap between Windows and Linux systems. By optimizing how Windows API calls are translated to Linux equivalents at the kernel level, Wine 11 delivers substantial speed gains that may finally make Linux a truly viable gaming platform. This development comes amid growing interest in alternative operating systems, with projects like [VitruvianOS](https://v-os.dev) drawing inspiration from BeOS to create more modern desktop Linux experiences that challenge traditional paradigms. Together, these initiatives represent a resurgence in operating system innovation that could eventually disrupt Microsoft's decades-long dominance in desktop computing.

### Venture Capital Doubles Down on AI
Kleiner Perkins has [raised $3.5 billion in fresh capital](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/with-3-5b-in-fresh-capital-kleiner-perkins-is-going-all-in-on-ai/) across two funds, signaling the continued investor enthusiasm for artificial intelligence despite broader market uncertainties. The legendary venture firm, known for early bets on Amazon and Google, has secured stakes in promising AI startups like Together AI, Harvey, and OpenEvidence, positioning itself at the forefront of the current tech revolution. Operating with a lean team of just five partners, Kleiner Perkins joins other major firms like Thrive Capital ($10 billion), General Catalyst, and Founders Fund ($6 billion) in amassing substantial war chests focused on AI investments. This fundraising surge demonstrates that despite concerns about valuations and slow exit markets, top-tier venture firms remain confident that transformative AI technologies will yield outsized returns.
