## Top Stories

A concerning [security breach has put millions of iPhones at risk](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/a-major-hacking-tool-has-leaked-online-putting-millions-of-iphones-at-risk-heres-what-you-need-to-know/) after powerful hacking tools known as "Coruna" and "DarkSword" have leaked online. Originally developed by Trenchant, a unit within U.S. defense contractor L3Harris, these tools somehow found their way to Russian spies and Chinese cybercriminals before parts were published on GitHub. DarkSword exploits can hack iPhones running iOS versions through 18.7, with security researchers describing the leaked code as "essentially plug-and-play," making it accessible for malicious actors to launch their own attacks. This incident demonstrates once again how even carefully guarded hacking tools can proliferate out of control, reminiscent of the 2017 NSA exploit leak that led to the WannaCry ransomware attack.

French AI company [Mistral has released an open-source text-to-speech model called Voxtral TTS](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/mistral-releases-a-new-open-source-model-for-speech-generation/), supporting nine languages and capable of adapting to custom voices with less than five seconds of sample audio. The model is designed for real-time performance with impressive responsiveness metrics and can run on edge devices like smartphones and smartwatches. This release positions Mistral to compete directly with ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and OpenAI in the voice AI market, with VP Pierre Stock noting that customers have been requesting speech capabilities. Mistral's approach emphasizes open-source flexibility and customization options for enterprises, allowing them to tune the model to their specific needs while maintaining human-like voice quality.

In a [landmark social media addiction trial](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/jury-finds-meta-and-youtube-negligent-in-landmark-social-media-addiction-trial/), a Los Angeles jury has found Meta and Google negligent in a case filed by Kaley, a 20-year-old who claimed Instagram and YouTube contributed to her anxiety, depression, and body dysmorphia. The jury awarded $3 million in compensatory damages (with Meta bearing 70% of the cost) plus additional punitive damages, bringing Meta's total to $4.2 million and YouTube's to $1.8 million. Evidence presented at trial showed Meta understood its platforms' addictive nature among teens and was actively researching the issue while using findings to increase engagement. This verdict, coming just after Meta lost a similar child safety lawsuit in New Mexico, establishes a precedent that social media companies bear responsibility for algorithmic harms, potentially opening the door to many more lawsuits.

[Apple has announced a significant update](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/apple-overhauls-its-app-developer-platform-with-100-new-metrics-more-tools/) to its App Store Connect service, providing developers with over 100 new metrics to track app performance, monetization, and subscription data. The overhaul includes new subscription reports that can be exported via API, cohort analysis tools, and peer group benchmarks that let developers compare themselves with competitors. The timing of this rollout is notable as AI threatens the traditional app store model – Apple seems to be positioning itself to enhance rather than replace its ecosystem, with reports suggesting the company plans to announce an AI-powered Siri at its upcoming developer conference that can complete tasks inside apps rather than making them obsolete.
